Tips

1. EXCERCISE: Physical activity is key in releasing your anger and tension. Go on a quick run, do some stretches or play outside for a bit.
2. WAIT: Waiting for a bit and getting into the right headspace before expressing your anger can be extremely helpful. Calmly expressing your concerns is always better than saying something when you're not thinking clearly.
3. 'I' STATEMENTS: Stick to using 'I' statements when you're expressing your frustration. This can help to avoid criticizing and placing blame, which will only create more tension.

Breathing Excercises

When you're angry, it's important to isolate yourself for a few minutes and focus on steady breathing. Breathe deeply about 3 to 4 times by inhaling for for about 4 seconds and then exhaling for about 8 seconds.

Recommended Foods

It's important to keep your blood sugar levels steady when you're angry. Some foods that are helpful for this are pumpkin seeds, blueberries, avocados, chia seeds, and cinammon
